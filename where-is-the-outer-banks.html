Where is the Outer Banks?

The Outer Banks, often referred to as OBX, is a popular vacation destination known for its stunning beaches, rich history, and scenic landscapes. But where exactly is the Outer Banks located?

Location of the Outer Banks

The Outer Banks is a string of barrier islands located off the coast of North Carolina in the United States. Stretching over 200 miles, these narrow islands separate the Atlantic Ocean from the mainland and are known for their unique geography and natural beauty.


Key Areas in the Outer Banks

The Outer Banks is divided into several key areas, each offering its own attractions and charm:

  • Northern Beaches: This area includes popular towns like Duck, Kitty Hawk, Kill Devil Hills, and Nags Head. Known for their sandy shores, these towns offer a variety of recreational activities, restaurants, and shops.
  • Roanoke Island: Located between the mainland and the barrier islands, Roanoke Island is home to the town of Manteo and the historic site of the Lost Colony, the first English settlement in the New World.
  • Hatteras Island: This island is known for its picturesque villages, including Rodanthe, Avon, and Hatteras. It's a prime spot for fishing, windsurfing, and exploring the Cape Hatteras National Seashore.
  • Ocracoke Island: Accessible only by ferry, boat, or plane, Ocracoke Island offers a more secluded and tranquil experience. The village of Ocracoke is known for its charming atmosphere and the famous Ocracoke Lighthouse.

Getting to the Outer Banks

Reaching the Outer Banks can be an adventure in itself. Here are the main ways to get there:

  • By Car: Many visitors drive to the Outer Banks via major highways such as US-158 and NC-12. The scenic drive offers beautiful views and the chance to explore different parts of the region.
  • By Ferry: Ferries operate between the mainland and certain islands, such as the Hatteras-Ocracoke ferry. These ferries provide a scenic and relaxing way to reach the Outer Banks.
  • By Air: The closest major airport is Norfolk International Airport (ORF) in Virginia, about a two-hour drive from the northern Outer Banks. Regional airports, such as Dare County Regional Airport (MEO), offer closer access for private planes and smaller commercial flights.

Conclusion

The Outer Banks is a unique and beautiful destination located off the coast of North Carolina. With its diverse regions, rich history, and variety of activities, it's no wonder that the Outer Banks is a beloved vacation spot for many. Whether you're driving in, taking a ferry, or flying, the journey to this coastal paradise is well worth the trip.
